Top 10 Must-Have Home Appliances to Upgrade Every Ghanaian Home

September 08, 2025

Modern Ghanaian Living Needs Home Appliances

With urbanisation accelerating in cities like Accra and Kumasi, Ghanaian households are embracing modern comforts. The shift toward dual-income families, full-time students, and gig workers means less time for domestic chores and a greater demand for appliances that simplify life. In this climate, having the right appliances can make all the difference, saving time, reducing stress, and improving well-being.

The Value of the Right Appliances

Imagine defrosting leftovers in seconds, doing laundry effortlessly, maintaining food freshness in erratic power situations, and staying cool during Harmattan or dry seasons. The right appliance not only enhances convenience but also improves energy efficiency, supports healthy living, and provides a reliable ROI over time.

IPMC Showroom: Your One-Stop Provider

All the essential appliances discussed here are available at the IPMC Showroom, East Legon, recognized as the must-have home appliances shop in Ghana. With trusted brands, expert advice, financing options, fast delivery, and excellent after-sales service, IPMC makes smart appliance ownership achievable and worry-free.

1. Refrigerator – The Food Preserver

Why It’s Essential

In Ghana’s warm climate, food spoils quickly if not stored properly. A refrigerator is the cornerstone of any home kitchen, allowing you to preserve perishable foods like meat, dairy, vegetables, and leftovers.

What to Look For

  • Energy-efficient models (star-rated)
  • Freezer combo (top or bottom)
  • Adjustable shelves and frost-free tech

Types of Fridges

  • Single-door compact fridges (100–200 L): Budget-friendly for small homes or student housing
  • Double-door fridges (300–600 L): Ideal for families, including pantry and freezer space
  • Side-by-side and smart models: Premium options with water/ice dispensers, external displays, and Wi-Fi
  • Inverter technology: Adjusts compressor speed based on need, lowering energy consumption by 20–30%, critical with Ghana’s erratic power supply
  • Energy-saving models: Designed to reduce electricity consumption while maintaining cooling efficiency

Choosing the Best Fridge

Opt for fridges with Energy Star or inverter ratings, suitable capacity, voltage tolerance, and warranty terms. IPMC staff helps with sizing advice and reaffirms genuine serials and parts.

2. Gas Cooker or Electric Stove – The Heart of the Kitchen

Why It’s Essential

A reliable cooking appliance is a necessity in every Ghanaian home. Whether you prefer traditional meals like jollof rice and banku or international dishes, a gas cooker or electric stove offers speed, safety, and efficiency.

What to Look For

  • Multiple burners
  • Oven integration
  • Auto-ignition and flame control
  • Safety valves (for gas cookers)

Options

  • Standing cookers: 4-burner stoves with gas/ceramic top, oven included
  • Tabletop burners: Space-saving for shared houses and hostels
  • Electric and induction cooktops: Quick heating, safer operation

Safety & Efficiency

Features like auto shut-off, safety valves, and ceramic heat plates are essential. Look for CE certification and high BTU output.

3. Washing Machine – For Effortless Laundry

Why It’s Essential

Hand-washing clothes can be time-consuming and labour-intensive, especially for large families. A washing machine takes the stress out of laundry days, saving time and reducing wear on clothing.

What to Look For

  • Top-load vs front-load
  • Load capacity (6kg–12kg)
  • Energy and water efficiency
  • Wash modes and spin speed

Fully vs Semi-Automatic

  • Fully automatic: Load once, select settings, and let it run—quiet, efficient, energy-smart
  • Semi-automatic: Easier to maintain, suitable for limited budgets, with manual water fills

IPMC offers models with useful features like spin dry, child locks, and multiple wash cycles.

Water & Time Savings

Machines use precise water and spin cycles to reduce waste. Outdoor drying is common, but faster drying in wet seasons due to strong spin cycles.

Buying Considerations

Choose capacity (6–10 kg), energy ratings, ease of service, and compatible stabilisers for longevity.

4. Microwave Oven – For Quick Meals and Reheating

Why It’s Essential

Microwaves are perfect for reheating food, making quick meals, defrosting frozen items, and preparing beverages. They’re ideal for students, busy professionals, and families alike.

What to Look For

  • Capacity (20L–30L)
  • Auto-cook settings
  • Timer and defrost features
  • Grill function (optional)

Features & Types

  • Solo microwaves: Basic reheating
  • Convection/grill models: Full-fledged cooking with baking/grilling
  • Sensor cooking: Automatically adjusts time based on moisture
  • Touch controls & safety locks: Ideal for family use

Featured brands include Samsung, LG, and Nasco.

Microwave Models at IPMC

  • 20–30 L capacity
  • Energy ratings that ensure efficient performance
  • Clean design and intuitive interfaces

5. Blender – The Versatile Kitchen Helper

Why It’s Essential

No Ghanaian kitchen is complete without a blender. From making smoothies, soups, and stews to blending tomatoes and peppers for your favourite shito, this tool is a daily necessity.

What to Look For

  • Power (wattage)
  • Durable stainless-steel blades
  • Extra jars or grinders
  • Easy-to-clean design

Everyday Utility

Blenders and food processors streamline:

  • Smoothies and juices
  • Soup and sauce preparation
  • Nut butter or flour from grains
  • Pureeing baby food

Power Matters

Blenders range from 300 W (simple tasks) to 1000 W+ (heavy-duty). Look for stainless steel blending jars for durability.

Brands at IPMC

Nasco, Samsung, and LG blenders come in single- and multi-speed models with easy-clean designs.

6. Air Conditioner or Fan – Beat the Ghana Heat

Why It’s Essential

Ghana’s weather can be extremely hot, especially during the Harmattan and dry seasons. A fan or air conditioner provides much-needed relief and improves sleep quality and comfort at home.

What to Look For

For Fans:

  • Adjustable height
  • Oscillation
  • Multiple speed settings
  • Quiet motor

For ACs:

  • Inverter models (for power savings)
  • Cooling capacity (1.0HP–2.0HP)
  • Remote control and timer
  • Energy efficiency rating

Options

  • Split ACs: Inverter types that adjust power to save energy
  • Window ACs: Compact, convenient
  • Standing/ceiling fans: Efficient for mild conditions
  • Tower fans: Portable and quiet

IPMC stocks Samsung, LG, and Midea with optional installation and smart controls.

7. Electric Kettle – Boil Water in Seconds

Why It’s Essential

Whether you’re making morning tea, baby formula, or instant noodles, an electric kettle saves time and energy compared to boiling water on a stove.

What to Look For

  • Auto shut-off
  • Capacity (1.5L–2L)
  • Stainless steel or BPA-free plastic
  • Cordless base

8. Television – Entertainment at Home

Why It’s Essential

TVs are more than entertainment; they’re a way to stay informed through news, sports, and educational content. With the rise of digital TV and streaming services, owning a television is now essential in most Ghanaian homes.

What to Look For

  • Screen size (32″, 40″, 50″+)
  • Smart TV features (Netflix, YouTube, apps)
  • HDMI and USB ports
  • HD or 4K resolution

Smart TV Features

  • Smart platforms: Roku, Android TV, Tizen
  • High resolution (1080p/4K)

Options at IPMC

Samsung and LG LED TVs with smart capabilities, sleek design, and access to Ghanaian and international streaming channels.

9. Iron or Steam Press – Look Sharp Every Day

Why It’s Essential

A good iron is key to looking well-dressed, whether for work, school, or church. It helps keep your clothes neat and presentable.

What to Look For

  • Non-stick soleplate
  • Temperature settings
  • Steam burst feature
  • Auto shut-off

Types of Irons

  • Steam irons: High heat and steam burst for stubborn creases
  • Dry irons: Lightweight, quick for light fabrics
  • Cordless irons: Portable convenience, but with limited run time

Features & Maintenance

Look for adjustable temperature, non-stick sole plates, and anti-calc features. Pair with sturdy ironing boards for best results.

10. Vacuum Cleaner – Clean Floors in No Time

Why It’s Essential

Gone are the days of sweeping with a broom alone. A vacuum cleaner picks up dust, pet hair, and allergens more effectively, especially in carpeted areas or large homes.

What to Look For

  • Suction power
  • Bagged vs bagless design
  • Cord length
  • Attachments (crevice tool, brush, etc.)

11. Water Dispenser – Easy Access to Clean Water

Clean Water at Home

Tap water quality issues make filters and dispensers essential. Hot and cold dispensers support tea, coffee, and safe drinking.

Features

  • Removable bottles or direct-connect
  • Hot safety lock, self-cleaning
  • Stylish stainless steel or ABS plastic models

IPMC offers premium dispensers with features ideal for homes and offices.

Tips for Ghanaian Electronics Shoppers

Electricity Considerations

Due to occasional load-shedding and voltage fluctuations, it’s crucial to invest in appliances that are energy-efficient and stable. Consider getting voltage stabilizers for sensitive electronics like fridges and TVs.

Budgeting for Appliances

Don’t buy everything at once. Start with core items like a fridge and cooking appliance, then gradually add others based on your budget. Many showrooms and e-commerce stores like IPMC Kart offer installment payment plans and seasonal discounts.

Conclusion

Equipping your home with the right appliances is not just about luxury; it’s about improving your quality of life. Whether it’s preserving food, staying cool, preparing meals faster, or keeping your clothes clean and crisp, these appliances are fundamental to any Ghanaian household.

Invest wisely, compare brands, check for energy ratings, and buy from reliable stores. And remember, every home is unique, so choose appliances that best suit your lifestyle and space.
